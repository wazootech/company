<core_models>

### Foundational Thinking
- **First Principles:** Break problems down to basic truths.
- **Jobs to Be Done (JTBD):** Focus on the outcome customers want ("hire" a
  product for a job).
- **Inversion:** Ask "What would guarantee failure?" and avoid those things.
- **Pareto Principle (80/20):** 80% of results come from 20% of efforts.
- **Theory of Constraints:** Find the one bottleneck limiting the system.
- **Second-Order Thinking:** Consider the effects of the effects.

### Understanding Buyers
- **Mere Exposure Effect:** Familiarity breeds preference.
- **Endowment Effect:** People value things more once they own them (Trial/Freemium).
- **IKEA Effect:** People value things more when they've put effort into building
  them.
- **Zero-Price Effect:** "Free" triggers a disproportionate psychological response.
- **Paradox of Choice:** Too many options paralyze decision-making.
- **Peak-End Rule:** People judge experiences by the peak moment and the end.
- **Zeigarnik Effect:** Unfinished tasks occupy the mind (Progress loops).

### Influence & Persuasion
- **Reciprocity:** Give value first to create a sense of obligation.
- **Social Proof:** People follow what others are doing (Bandwagon Effect).
- **Authority Bias:** People defer to experts and credentials.
- **Mimetic Desire:** People want what others want.
- **Loss Aversion:** The pain of loss is twice as strong as the joy of gain.
- **Anchoring:** The first number seen heavily influences judgment.

</core_models>
